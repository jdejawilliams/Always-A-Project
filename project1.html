Overview

Heat into Electricity

Collaborated in a team to design and build a thermodynamic system that converts heat into electrical energy via steam generation and turbine motion. The project challenged us to apply real-world engineering constraints — material selection, pressure management, and energy conversion efficiency — in a hands-on build environment.

Led design iterations, material selection, and component assembly, as well as conducted testing to evaluate system performance and troubleshoot inefficiencies across the steam loop.

Process & Challenges

Engineering the Loop

The core challenge was designing a sealed steam loop that could sustain pressure long enough to drive turbine rotation and generate measurable electrical output. Material compatibility with high-temperature steam required careful selection and iterative testing.

Troubleshooting inefficiencies in the heat exchange and pressure regulation stages was the most technically demanding aspect of the project, ultimately improving system output by refining seal geometry and boiler insulation.
