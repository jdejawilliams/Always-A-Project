Overview

From CAD to Cast Metal

A full manufacturing pipeline project — starting with CAD modeling in Fusion 360, through resin 3D printing for prototyping, into crucible metal casting, and finishing with CNC machining for surface quality. The Lexus logo served as the target geometry, demanding precision at every stage.

The project demonstrated how different manufacturing methods chain together, and where each process introduces its own tolerancing and finishing challenges.

Fusion 360 Resin 3D Printing Metal Casting CNC Machining GD&T DFM
Manufacturing Process
01
CAD Modeling
Designed the Lexus logo in Fusion 360, applying manufacturability principles for casting and machining downstream.
02
Resin 3D Print
Fabricated prototype using resin 3D printing to validate geometry, fit, and draft angles before committing to casting.
03
Crucible Casting
Created a mold and poured metal via crucible casting to produce the solid final part with full geometry.
04
CNC Finishing
Performed CNC machining operations to refine features and achieve a high-quality surface finish on the cast part.
